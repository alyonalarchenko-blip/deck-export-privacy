Deck Export

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

Deck Export ("the plugin") is a Figma plugin that exports selected frames to Google Slides, PDF, and PPTX. This page explains what data the plugin accesses, how it is used, and how it is stored.

What data we access

To export your designs to Google Slides, the plugin requests permission to access:

  • Your Google account email address — used only to show you which account is connected.
  • Google Slides — to create and populate a presentation with your exported frames.
  • Google Drive — limited to the files the plugin itself creates, so the resulting presentation can be saved to your Drive.

The plugin does not access, read, or modify any other files in your Google Drive, and does not read your Figma files beyond the frame(s) you explicitly select for export.

How authentication works

When you connect your Google account, sign-in happens directly through Google's own OAuth screen — the plugin never sees or stores your Google password.

After you sign in, Google issues an access token. This token is held briefly by a backend relay (a Cloudflare Worker) only long enough to hand it back to the Figma plugin — typically a few seconds, and never longer than 10 minutes. After that window, or once the plugin retrieves it, the token is deleted and cannot be retrieved again.

Figma itself stores your session (so you don't have to reconnect every time) using Figma's built-in clientStorage, which is scoped to your own machine and account — not accessible to us or to anyone else.

What we don't do

  • We don't store your design content, exported images, or presentation contents on any server.
  • We don't sell, share, or use your data for advertising.
  • We don't track usage analytics tied to your identity.
  • We don't retain access or refresh tokens beyond what's needed to keep your session working — nothing is logged permanently.

Data retention

Authentication data passed through the backend relay is stored for a maximum of 10 minutes and is deleted immediately after use or upon expiry, whichever comes first. No design files, exported assets, or presentation content ever pass through or are stored on the backend — the export happens directly between Figma and Google's APIs.

Third parties

The plugin communicates only with Google's official APIs (Slides, Drive, OAuth) and its own backend relay. No other third-party services receive your data.

Revoking access

You can disconnect the plugin at any time from within Figma, or revoke its access entirely from your Google Account permissions page.
